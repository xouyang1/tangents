<h2>Microplastic Protection Over Filter Life</h2>
<p>Estimated degradation curve · peer-reviewed data & manufacturer specs</p>
</div>

<div class="wf-timeline-grid">

<div class="wf-timeline-card wf-baseline">
<h3 style="color: #a09890;">Pentair Rocean</h3>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-good"><div class="wf-phase-label">0–25% (0–100 gal)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Fresh carbon at peak. Pump pressure ensures consistent contact time. Particulate Class I at 99.8%.</div></div>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-good"><div class="wf-phase-label">25–50% (100–200 gal)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Strong. Long-life filter (390 gal) means slow saturation curve. Pump advantage: pressure-driven flow doesn't degrade like gravity.</div></div>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-ok"><div class="wf-phase-label">50–75% (200–290 gal)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">LED turns orange at &#126;350 gal. Carbon adsorption sites filling. Chemical contaminants likely declining before particulate trapping.</div></div>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-warn"><div class="wf-phase-label">75–100% (290–390 gal)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Same re-release dynamics as all carbon media. LED goes red at capacity. Replace promptly — usage-based tracking is the best indicator of the four systems.</div></div>
<div class="wf-timeline-note">Key advantage: 390-gal life + pump pressure + usage-based LED made this the most user-friendly system for timely replacement. The UX gap from Rocean to any successor is real.</div>
</div>

<div class="wf-timeline-card">
<h3 style="color: #6b8f71;">AquaTru Classic</h3>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-good"><div class="wf-phase-label">0–50% (0–12 mo)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">RO membrane at full effectiveness. Mechanical size exclusion — no degradation of microplastic protection. All particles ≥0.0001 µm blocked.</div></div>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-good"><div class="wf-phase-label">50–75% (12–18 mo)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Membrane stable. Flow slows as membrane fouls — but fouling actually tightens rejection. Microplastic protection improves, not degrades.</div></div>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-ok"><div class="wf-phase-label">75–100% (18–24 mo)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Flow noticeably slower. Microplastic barrier intact. Replace for flow rate, not safety. Pre-filters (carbon) may saturate but sit upstream of RO.</div></div>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-good"><div class="wf-phase-label">Re-release risk</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Zero. Particles rejected to wastewater, not accumulated in media. No saturation curve for microplastics.</div></div>
<div class="wf-timeline-note">Key insight: Only system where microplastic protection doesn't degrade over filter life. The physics are fundamentally different from carbon adsorption.</div>
</div>

<div class="wf-timeline-card">
<h3 style="color: #7b8ba6;">Clearly Filtered</h3>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-good"><div class="wf-phase-label">0–25% (0–25 gal / &#126;1 mo)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Fresh media at peak. Composite + carbon pores unoccupied. Microplastic trapping near 99.99% claim.</div></div>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-good"><div class="wf-phase-label">25–50% (25–50 gal / &#126;2 mo)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Still strong. Sites filling but ample capacity. Flow beginning gradual decline.</div></div>
<div class="wf-phase wf-phase-ok"><div class="wf-phase-label">50–75% (50–75 gal / &#126;3 mo)</div><div class="wf-phase-desc">Sites increasingly saturated. Chemical removal declining. Microplastic trapping likely adequate but unverified at this stage.</div></div>

<strong>For NYC municipal water:</strong> All four systems handle lead, THMs, and PFAS effectively. For microplastics: AquaTru is in a different physical tier (RO membrane).
